Entry & Customs

  • US, Canada, UK, Australia & most EU passport holders: no visa needed. You'll get a Tourist Card on arrival for $10 – $15 USD.
  • Have small USD bills ready ($5, $10, $20) — they often don't have change for $50 or $100.
  • You may be asked to show a return ticket to prove you're leaving within 3 months. Avianca will not let you check in without one.
  • When asked for your address in Nicaragua, provide: El Remanzo, Rivas, Nicaragua.
Drones & binoculars are not permitted into Nicaragua and will be confiscated by customs unless you have a government permit. Leave them at home.

Cash & Money

  • Nicaragua's currency is the córdoba, but USD is accepted almost everywhere.
  • Bring about $500 USD in small bills ($5, $10, $20). Most local spots can't break $50 or $100.
  • ATMs are available in San Juan del Sur.
  • Entry tax at Managua airport: $10 – $15 USD (have exact change ready).

Health & Vaccines

Consult your doctor about the following recommended vaccines:

  • Hepatitis A & B
  • Typhoid
  • Tetanus

Malaria risk is extremely low — most travelers do not take malaria medication.

This is a remote location. The nearest hospital is 30+ minutes away. If you have severe allergies, carry an EpiPen and let the group know where you keep it.

Avoiding Surf Rash

Knees, hips and ribs can get sore from rubbing against the board while paddling. Rashguards and surf pants protect against this. A 1–2mm neoprene top gives extra protection without being too hot in the tropics.

About San Juan del Sur

San Juan del Sur is a charming village on Nicaragua's Pacific coast, sitting between the southern beaches (Hermosa, Yankee, Remanso) and the northern beaches (Maderas). The waves here are some of the most consistent on earth — perfect for beginners and intermediates to progress.

The town has preserved its unique cultural charm. Explore local markets, meet warm locals, and visit one of the most magnificent Jesus statues in the world on top of San Juan's hill.
